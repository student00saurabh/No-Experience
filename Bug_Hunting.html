Bug Hunting

Bug hunting, also known as bug bounty hunting, involves finding and reporting software bugs, especially security vulnerabilities, in applications, systems, or websites. Unlike traditional software testing, bug hunting is often conducted by individuals or independent security researchers outside of the organization that owns the software.

Darknet

Definition:

The Darknet refers to a specific part of the internet that is intentionally hidden and accessible only with special software, configurations, or authorization. It is a subset of the Deep Web, which encompasses all parts of the internet not indexed by traditional search engines.

Characteristics:

  • Privacy and Anonymity: Darknets are designed to offer high levels of privacy and anonymity. They often use encryption and anonymizing technologies to hide users' identities and activities.
  • Specialized Networks: Common darknet networks include Tor (The Onion Router), I2P (Invisible Internet Project), and Freenet. These networks require users to install specific software or configure their systems to access them.
  • Usage: The Darknet is used for a variety of purposes, some legal and some illegal. It can be a haven for whistleblowers, journalists, and activists in oppressive regimes, as well as a marketplace for illegal activities.

Darkweb

Definition:

The Darkweb is a term used to describe websites and content that exist on darknets. It is a smaller, specific part of the Darknet where websites, marketplaces, forums, and other services are hosted.
